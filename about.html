First chapters

It all started back in 1996. I was born in a provincial settlement called N, at a time when the appearance of a computer as a desktop device was only at dawn. From early childhood, and even in school days, I was a minor perfectionist and just a child who always sought to develop and receive more and more information. He led an active lifestyle and never sat still. I always liked to structure everything, to look for answers to the questions I received - on my own, without the help of other people. I devoted enough time to study, in particular to the exact sciences, as I understood that I wanted to connect my future with computer technology. After all, I dreamed of a computer ..

My parents were ordinary workers, without any privileges or anything. They were not strong in the field of trends and did not really understand what the future was behind these “machines”. I somehow started a conversation on the topic of buying a computer for me, but they flatly refused. From that moment on, I put aside all my savings to buy a computer. After a long time, being in the ninth grade, nevertheless, I managed to collect and purchase it. It was something..

By the time university was just around the corner, one of the more traditional courses didn't suit me. I was just sure that there would be no difficulties in entering the “Faculty of Information Technology”. And I thought, wow.. this is quite a promising specialty, and I think I will not lose. Therefore, while my classmates rushed about and ran about different educational institutions and applied for different specialties, I was only in one direction, which I wrote about above.

Programming my life

As you understand, a student career is not the most rewarding path. Here he is not only greatly underestimated, but completely ignored from a financial point of view. It took me almost 3 years to understand that I would not work as a computer engineer (system administrator). In addition, it was not millet so. I realized that I just did not want to sink into the wilds of the routine of monotonous installation of workstations, connecting local networks and banal printer repairs, it would be too boring for me.

So, I dropped out for a fairly long period of time in search of myself, while simultaneously working in all sorts of jobs where I did not get any pleasure. I thought for a long time, and went to this question. What if I could still find myself in the IT field as a web developer, and it's not just starting over, but it's also a challenge for me, because I have programming experience in C, C ++, C #, Visual Basic at university? Yes, that's a cool idea! Since then I have started positioning myself as a (professional) web developer.

This site is meant for me in all these forms. You will find my experience, as well as random thoughts, alternative things that I like, and so on. So yes, sit back, cool off and explore it!
